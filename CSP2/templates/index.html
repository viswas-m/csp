Procedures to purify water

Boiling

A common method that kills bacteria, viruses, and other pathogens.Bring the water to a rolling boil for 3-5 minutes. Let cool before drinking.

Chlorination

This involves adding a small amount of chlorine to the water, which kills most bacteria and viruses. Chlorination is an older, yet effective technique often used in emergencies when other methods are not readily available2.

Filtration

This involves passing water through a filter to remove impurities. There are many types of water filters available, including activated carbon filters and ceramic filters

RO Water Plant

A Reverse Osmosis (RO) Water Plant is a facility that purifies water. The process involves pre-treatment of water, pressurization, and then forcing the water through a semi-permeable membrane. This membrane allows water molecules to pass through but blocks larger molecules like contaminants and dissolved salts. The purified water is then collected for use. However, the process also produces waste water containing the concentrated contaminants.
